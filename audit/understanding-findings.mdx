title: "Understanding findings"
description: "How to read severity, expected vs actual, config source, and reasoning."
---

Every finding in an audit answers four questions:

1. **What went wrong?** - the finding type and description.
2. **How serious is it?** - the severity.
3. **What was expected?** - PX's calculated value, with the source.
4. **Why did PX expect that?** - the reasoning string.

Once you internalize these four, you can triage findings quickly.

## Severity

Three levels:

- **Error** - a real discrepancy with a definite financial impact. Examples: missing payment on a booking, ledger out of balance, commission rate disagrees with config.
- **Warning** - something looks off but may have an explanation. Examples: pet-related notes but no pet fee, payment recorded after departure, inferred catalog rule applied with wide tolerance.
- **Info** - context for human review. Examples: a booking matches a manual exception you set up, a tax was suppressed because of Airbnb remittance.

Filter by severity in the Findings Breakdown grid above the results table.

## Expected, actual, and difference

Each finding has:

- **Expected amount** - the value PX calculated.
- **Actual amount** - the value recorded on the booking.
- **Difference** - signed (positive when under-collected, negative when over-collected).

The summary card at the top of the audit aggregates these into the **Verdict** - the net direction across all findings.

## Config source

A short label identifying which configuration drove the expectation:

| Source | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `account_tax_config` | The tax came from your PMS's account-level taxes. |
| `listing_tax_config` | The tax came from a listing-level override in your PMS. |
| `channel_override` | A user channel sync override decided whether the tax applies. |
| `airbnb_remittance` | The Airbnb tax remittance setting suppressed or required this tax. |
| `business_model` | The business model drove the expectation (commissions, recurring charges). |
| `pmc_config.commission_setup.confirmed` | The confirmed commission rule. |
| `pmc_config.commission_setup.canceled` | The canceled commission rule. |
| `pmc_config.trust_account_setup.income_charges[N]` | A specific recurring income charge. |
| `ownerrez_catalog` | The OwnerRez Catalog (for OwnerRez accounts). |
| `derived` | PX inferred the expectation from booking data alone. |

This tells you exactly where to look if you want to change the expectation.

## Reasoning

A short string explaining how PX arrived at the expected amount. Example:

```
expected = 0.085 * (accommodation_fare + cleaning_fee)
         = 0.085 * (1200.00 + 150.00)
         = 0.085 * 1350.00
         = 114.75
```

When the reasoning is shown inline, the formula uses real values from the booking. This is usually enough to understand whether the issue is the booking or the configuration.

## Finding types you will see most often

### Tax

- **MISSING_TAX** - PX expected a tax that is not on the booking.
- **UNEXPECTED_TAX** - the booking has a tax that is not in your configuration.
- **TAX_AMOUNT_MISMATCH** - the tax is present but the amount is wrong.
- **TAX_CALCULATION_MISMATCH** (OwnerRez) - the tax math does not reconcile against catalog rules.

### Fee

- **MISSING_FEE** - the business model expects a fee that is not on the booking.
- **FEE_AMOUNT_MISMATCH** - the fee is present but the amount is wrong.
- **MISSING_PET_FEE** / **PET_FEE_MISMATCH** - pet-specific.
- **MISSING_EXTRA_GUEST_FEE** / **EXTRA_GUEST_FEE_MISMATCH** - extra guest specific.
- **NOTES_SUGGEST_PET** / **PET_FEE_NEEDS_REVIEW** - PX detected pet keywords in notes but no pet fee was charged.
- **HIDDEN_OTA_FEE** - Airbnb-specific. The Airbnb total does not equal the expected total.

### Commission

- **MISSING_COMMISSION** - commission expected but absent.
- **COMMISSION_MISMATCH** - commission present but the amount is wrong.
- **COMMISSION_RATE_DRIFT** (OwnerRez) - the same channel has multiple different rates across bookings.

### Payment

- **MISSING_PAYMENT** - balance > $0.01 but the booking is not flagged unpaid.
- **OVERPAYMENT** - the guest paid more than the balance.
- **NEGATIVE_BALANCE** - the booking shows a negative balance.
- **PAYMENTS_TOTAL_MISMATCH** - sum of payments does not equal recorded total.
- **REFUNDS_TOTAL_MISMATCH** - sum of refunds does not equal recorded refund total.
- **ORPHAN_REFUND** - a refund without a corresponding payment.
- **DUPLICATE_PAYMENT** - two payments that look like the same transaction.

### Booking integrity

- **REVENUE_INTEGRITY_MISMATCH** - sum of charges does not equal total amount.
- **CHARGE_SPLIT_MISMATCH** - owner amount + commission amount on a charge does not equal the charge amount.
- **ZERO_RENT_CHARGES** - active booking with no accommodation charge.
- **MISSING_GUEST** - booking with no guest record.
- **OVERLAPPING_BOOKINGS** - two confirmed bookings on the same property overlap in dates.
- **PHANTOM_BOOKING** - past departure, $0 paid, still active status.

### Ledger and accounting

- **LEDGER_BALANCE_MISMATCH** - generated ledger does not balance.
- **OWNER_PAYOUT_MISMATCH** - calculated owner payout does not match the recorded payout.
- **RECOGNITION_DATE_ANOMALY** - a charge is recognized on the wrong date per the business model.

### Catalog

- **CATALOG_EMPTY** (OwnerRez only) - no confirmed catalog rules. Audit short-circuits.

## How to triage a finding

1. Read the **reasoning** string - it tells you the formula.
2. Check the **config source** - that points to where the expectation came from.
3. Decide:
   - If the configuration is wrong, edit it. The next audit clears the finding.
   - If the booking is wrong, fix it in the PMS. The next audit re-checks.
   - If the booking is a one-off legitimate exception, **dismiss** the finding from the property detail page.

Most findings fall into one of these three buckets. Findings that cannot be triaged in a few minutes usually point to a deeper configuration issue worth investigating.

## False positives

False positives happen most often when:

- The PMS catalog is incomplete (very common on OwnerRez before catalog confirmation).
- Channel sync overrides are missing for a channel that does not actually carry the tax.
- Airbnb tax remittance is not configured.
- A business model has a typo in commission percent or recurring charge category.

In all these cases, fixing the configuration is the right answer - dismissing the finding only hides the symptom.
