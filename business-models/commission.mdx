---
title: "Commission"
description: "Configure confirmed and canceled commission rates, recognition, owner VAT/GST, and channel overrides."
---

The Commission section of a business model defines what the PMC takes off the top of each booking. PX validates calculated commission against your PMS during audits and flags any drift.

## Three sub-sections

- **Confirmed reservations** - the standard path.
- **Canceled reservations** - cancellation-specific rules (often a lower rate).
- **Minimum commission** - a floor that ensures you never earn less than a fixed amount per booking.

## Confirmed reservations

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Enable** | Turn on commission for confirmed bookings. |
| **Commission %** | Default rate, for example `20` for 20%. |
| **Recognition** | When commission is recognized in the GL: `check_in`, `check_out`, or `nightly` (spread across the stay). |
| **Owner tax** | Optional. Charge the owner an extra VAT or GST on top of commission. Applied at the same recognition date as commission. |
| **Channel conditions** | Per-channel overrides - useful when channels have different settlement terms. |

### Owner tax fields

If you enable owner tax:

- **Name** - what to call the line in the GL (`Owner GST 5%`, `Owner VAT 20%`).
- **Category** - typically `owner_gst` or `owner_vat`. PX uses this to group similar lines on owner statements.
- **Description** - free-text notes.
- **Value %** - the tax rate.

Recognition for owner tax is fixed: it always uses the accommodation fare's recognition date, regardless of the commission recognition you chose.

### Channel conditions

Click **Add channel condition** to create overrides:

- **Channels** - one or more channel slugs (`airbnb2`, `bookingCom`, `vrbo`, `direct`).
- **Commission %** - the rate for those channels.

If a booking's channel matches a condition, that condition's rate replaces the default. Otherwise the default applies.

## Canceled reservations

Same fields as confirmed, with one difference:

- **Recognition** options are `cancellation_date` (when the booking was canceled) or `original_check_in` (when the booking would have arrived).

A typical setup is a lower rate (5-10% instead of 20%) recognized on `cancellation_date` because the work is done as soon as you process the cancellation.

If you do not charge commission on cancellations, leave **Enable** off.

## Minimum commission

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Enable** | Whether to enforce a floor. |
| **Amount** | Numeric floor (for example `25`). |
| **Currency** | Defaults to USD. |

When the calculated commission is below the floor, PX bumps it up to the floor amount. The audit reports the minimum as the expected value when this happens, so you see the difference clearly.

## Recognition timing in practice

- **`check_in`** - revenue lands when guests arrive. Easy to reconcile against bank deposits.
- **`check_out`** - revenue lands when guests leave. Useful when refunds are common between booking and check-in.
- **`nightly`** - amount divided across nights and recognized day by day. Smooths long stays across month-end.
- **`cancellation_date`** - cancellations recognized when processed.
- **`original_check_in`** - cancellations recognized when arrival would have been (uncommon).

The audit's `RECOGNITION_DATE_ANOMALY` finding fires when the GL date does not match what your business model says it should be.

## Channel slugs

PX uses these slugs to identify channels:

| Slug | Channel |
|---|---|
| `airbnb2` | Airbnb |
| `bookingCom` | Booking.com |
| `vrbo` | Vrbo |
| `expedia` | Expedia |
| `direct` | Direct booking |
| `homeAway` | HomeAway (legacy) |

If your PMS uses a slug PX does not recognize, the audit treats it as `direct` and applies the default commission rate.

## Audit findings related to commission

- **MISSING_COMMISSION** - PX expected commission but the booking has none.
- **COMMISSION_MISMATCH** - calculated commission differs from what is recorded.
- **COMMISSION_RATE_DRIFT** (OwnerRez) - the same channel appears with multiple different rates across bookings, suggesting a mid-period change.

Each finding includes the **expected** amount, the **actual** amount, the **difference**, and a **reasoning** string showing PX's calculation.
