---
title: "Trust account"
description: "Recurring income and expense charges, with PMC vs owner allocation."
---

Trust account charges are recurring postings PX makes for every reservation. Use them to capture cleaning, linen, maintenance reserves, owner-paid insurance, and other recurring items - things that always happen but live outside the standard rental commission calculation.

## Two buckets

- **Income charges** - things owed to the PMC or vendors. They credit the trust account.
- **Expense charges** - things owed back to owners or paid out of trust. They debit the trust account.

Each charge is independent. You can have as many of each as you need.

## Adding a charge

Click **Add charge** in either bucket to open the dialog. Every charge has the same set of fields.

### Identification

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Display label like `Cleaning fee`, `Linen replenishment`, `Maintenance reserve`. |
| **Category** | Bucket for reporting and excluded-items logic. |
| **Description** | Free-text notes. |

### Amount

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Amount type** | `Fixed` - you set the value here. `Variable` - PX reads the value from the matching PMS line item. |
| **Amount** | The numeric value (when fixed). |
| **Amount is percent** | Toggle to interpret the amount as a percent of the matched line item rather than an absolute number. |
| **Per** | `stay` - one occurrence per booking. `night` - per-night multiplier. |

Use **Variable** when the amount comes from your PMS - cleaning fees that vary by listing, for example. PX matches the line item by category and uses whatever amount the PMS recorded.

### Recognition

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Recognition** | When the charge is recognized: `check_in`, `check_out`, or `nightly`. |
| **Canceled recognition** | Recognition for canceled bookings. Leave blank to inherit from the confirmed setting. |

### Allocation

This is the trickiest part. **Allocation** determines who is on the receiving side of the posting.

- **`vendor`** - the charge goes to a third party (a cleaner, a maintenance company). The vendor field captures their share.
- **`owner`** - the charge goes back to the owner.
- **`both`** - the charge is split between vendor and owner (and the PMC's share is implied by what is left over).

When allocation is `both`, both vendor and owner values are used:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Vendor value / vendor is percent** | The vendor's share, in fixed amount or percent. |
| **Owner value / owner is percent** | The owner's share, in fixed amount or percent. |
| **PMC share** | Implied (100% minus vendor and owner when using percent). |

The math constraint when using percentages: PMC + vendor + owner must equal 100%.

### Owner VAT/GST

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Charge owner VAT/GST** | Optional. Apply a VAT or GST on top of this charge, billed to the owner. |
| **Name / Category / Description** | Same as commission's owner tax fields. |
| **Value %** | The tax rate. |

This is independent of the commission's owner tax setting - you can apply VAT/GST on commission only, on a charge only, or on both.

### Conditions

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Excluded channels** | Channels where this charge does not apply. Leave empty to apply to all channels. |

Useful for channel-specific behavior. For example, exclude `airbnb2` from a cleaning fee charge if Airbnb collects cleaning fees as part of its host fee.

## Examples

**Cleaning kept by an external cleaner.** Type: expense. Amount: variable, per stay. Allocation: vendor (100%). The PMC and owner see no impact - cleaning passes through to the cleaner.

**Maintenance reserve, $50 per stay, owner-funded.** Type: expense. Amount: $50 fixed, per stay. Allocation: owner. The owner's payout is reduced by $50 each stay, accumulating in trust for repairs.

**Linen fee, $10 per night, PMC-retained.** Type: income. Amount: $10 fixed, per night. Allocation: leave defaults so the PMC retains the charge.

**Resort fee, split 60% owner / 40% PMC.** Type: income. Amount: variable, per stay. Allocation: both. Owner value: 60%, owner is percent. Vendor value: 0%. PMC share: 40% (implied).

## Audit findings related to trust account

- **MISSING_FEE** - the business model expects a charge but the booking does not have one.
- **FEE_AMOUNT_MISMATCH** - the charge is present but the amount differs from the model's calculation.
- **MISSING_PET_FEE / EXTRA_GUEST_FEE** - specialized findings for guest-driven fees.
- **NOTES_SUGGEST_PET / PET_FEE_NEEDS_REVIEW** - PX detected pet-related keywords in the booking notes but no pet fee was charged.

These findings include the booking ID, the expected amount, the actual amount, and reasoning that explains how the expected value was computed.

## When trust account becomes essential

Trust account configuration is what enables PX's ledger balance and owner statement reconciliation findings. Without trust account charges set up:

- Audits still detect data integrity issues (zero rent, payment anomalies, missing payments).
- Audits will not detect that an owner statement is off by $50 because a maintenance reserve was never posted.

The latter category is usually what catches the most expensive operational mistakes, so investing in trust account configuration pays back quickly.
